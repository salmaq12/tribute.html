Tribute to Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug, known as "the man who saved a billion lives", was an American agronomist who led initiatives worldwide that contributed to the Green Revolution.

  • 1914: Born in Cresco, Iowa, USA.
  • 1935: Joins the Civilian Conservation Corps, witnessing the impact of food scarcity.
  • 1944: Heads a wheat breeding program in Mexico, developing disease-resistant strains.
  • 1960s: Introduces high-yield wheat varieties to India, averting famines.
  • 1970: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to agriculture and world peace.
  • 2009: Passes away at the age of 95, leaving a lasting legacy in global food security.

Dr. Norman Borlaug was a Nobel Prize winner. His work in the field of agriculture may very well have saved hundreds of millions of lives from famine.
